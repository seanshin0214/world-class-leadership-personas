## Prospecting & Outreach

```markdown
IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP):

COMPANY ATTRIBUTES:
- Industry: SaaS, FinTech, Healthcare
- Size: 500-5000 employees
- Revenue: $50M-$500M
- Geography: North America, EU
- Tech stack: Salesforce, AWS, Slack
- Pain points: Manual processes, compliance needs

BUYING COMMITTEE:
- Champion: Loves your product, internal advocate
- Economic Buyer: Budget authority (VP, C-level)
- Decision Maker: Final say (may be different from EB)
- Technical Buyer: IT, Security (evaluates feasibility)
- End Users: Day-to-day users (influence decision)
- Blocker: Opposes change (competitor fan, status quo)
```

## Discovery

```markdown
SPIN SELLING:

SITUATION:
- Understand current state
- "Walk me through your current process for [X]"
- "What tools are you using?"
- "How many people are involved?"

PROBLEM:
- Uncover pain points
- "What challenges are you facing with that?"
- "How often does this happen?"
- "What's the impact?"

IMPLICATION:
- Amplify pain (make it urgent)
- "What happens if this isn't solved?"
- "How does this affect your team's productivity?"
- "What's the cost (time, money, morale)?"

NEED-PAYOFF:
- Get them to sell themselves
- "If you could solve this, what would change?"
- "How would that impact your goals?"
- "What would that be worth to you?"

---

DISCOVERY CALL STRUCTURE (60 min):

OPENING (5 min):
- Build rapport ("How's your week?")
- Set agenda ("Here's what I'd like to cover...")
- Get permission ("Does that work for you?")

BACKGROUND (10 min):
- Company overview
- Role & responsibilities
- Current state

PROBLEM EXPLORATION (25 min):
- SPIN questions
- Deep dive into pain points
- Quantify impact (numbers!)

VISION OF SOLUTION (10 min):
- "If you had a magic wand..."
- Desired outcome
- Success metrics

NEXT STEPS (10 min):
- Recap key points
- Propose next steps ("I'll send a tailored demo...")
- Schedule follow-up
- Confirm stakeholders ("Who else should be involved?")

---

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS CHEAT SHEET:

BUDGET:
- "What's your budget for solving this?"
- "How do you typically allocate budget for [category]?"

AUTHORITY:
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your typical approval process?"

NEED:
- "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is this?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this by [timeframe]?"

TIMELINE:
- "When do you need this implemented?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"

COMPETITION:
- "Are you evaluating other solutions?"
- "What do you like/dislike about them?"

SUCCESS:
- "How will you measure success?"
- "What metrics matter most?"
```

## Demo Best Practices

```markdown
BEFORE THE DEMO:

RESEARCH:
- Review discovery notes
- Check LinkedIn (recent posts, interests)
- Look at their website/product
- Identify use cases

CUSTOMIZE:
- Use their company name, logo
- Prepare relevant data examples
- Tailor to their pain points

SET AGENDA:
Email 24 hours before:
"Looking forward to our demo tomorrow! Here's what we'll cover:
1. Quick recap of your goals
2. Demo of [feature A, B, C] specific to your use case
3. Q&A
4. Next steps

Please invite anyone who should be there."

---

DURING THE DEMO:

OPENING (5 min):
- Introductions (all attendees)
- Recap discovery ("You mentioned you struggle with...")
- Set expectations ("I'll show X, Y, Z. Please stop me with questions anytime")

DEMO (30 min):
- Show, don't tell (click through, not slides)
- Start with biggest pain point
- Use their terminology (not yours)
- Pause for questions every 5-10 min
- "Does this make sense so far?"

STRUCTURE:
1. Problem statement
2. Show solution
3. Outcome/benefit
4. Repeat for next feature

AVOID:
âŒ Feature dump (showing everything)
âŒ Going too fast (let them absorb)
âŒ Talking non-stop (engage them)
âœ… Focus on 3-5 key features (not 50)
âœ… Use case-based narrative
âœ… Pause for questions

CLOSE (10 min):
- Recap how you address their needs
- Check concerns ("Any questions or concerns?")
- Next steps ("I'll send a proposal by Friday")
- Timeline ("When would you like to make a decision?")
```

## Proposal & Negotiation

```markdown
PROPOSAL STRUCTURE:

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
- 1 page overview
- Problem statement
- Proposed solution
- ROI/value
- Investment

SITUATION:
- Current state (their pain points)
- Impact of not solving

SOLUTION:
- How your product addresses each pain point
- Key features & benefits
- Implementation plan

PRICING:
- Tiered options (Good, Better, Best)
- Breakdown: Software, implementation, support
- Annual vs monthly (show savings for annual)

Example:
| Tier | Users | Price/Year | Features |
|------|-------|------------|----------|
| Starter | 25 | $10K | Core features |
| Professional | 100 | $35K | Core + Advanced |
| Enterprise | Unlimited | $75K | All features + dedicated CSM |

CASE STUDIES:
- 2-3 similar companies
- Results (metrics)
- Testimonials

NEXT STEPS:
- Timeline
- Who does what
- Key milestones

---

NEGOTIATION TACTICS:

ANCHORING:
- Start high (they'll negotiate down)
- "Our standard price is $100K, but for [reason], I can do $85K"

BUNDLING:
- Include extras (training, premium support)
- "If you sign by end of month, I'll throw in..."

DISCOUNTING RULES:
- Never discount >20% (devalues product)
- Always tie to concession ("If you sign today...")
- Annual prepay = discount (improves cash flow)

COMMON OBJECTIONS:

"Too expensive":
- Reframe as ROI: "What's the cost of not solving this?"
- Break down per user: "$75K/year = $750/user/year = $2/day"
- Compare to alternatives: "What would it cost to build in-house?"

"Need to think about it":
- Uncover real objection: "What specifically do you need to think about?"
- Timeline: "When do you expect to make a decision?"
- Risk: "What happens if you wait?"

"Your competitor is cheaper":
- Value differentiation: "What's driving your decision - price or [value]?"
- Feature comparison: "Let's compare apples to apples..."
- Testimonials: "Here's why [Customer] chose us over them..."

---

CLOSING TECHNIQUES:

ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE:
- Act as if they've decided
- "Should we start implementation in Q1 or Q2?"
- "Will you need 50 or 100 licenses?"

ALTERNATIVE CLOSE:
- Two options (both assume sale)
- "Starter or Professional tier?"
- "Annual or monthly billing?"

URGENCY CLOSE:
- Deadline (real, not fake)
- "This pricing expires end of quarter"
- "We have limited implementation slots in Q1"

TRIAL CLOSE:
- Test readiness
- "If we can address [concern], are you ready to move forward?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you?"

DIRECT CLOSE:
- Simply ask
- "Shall we get the paperwork started?"
- "Are you ready to move forward?"
```

```markdown

- Introductions (CSM, implementation team)

- Quick wins

- Data migration
- Integrations
- Training sessions

- Monitor usage metrics
- Weekly check-ins
- Address issues proactively

---

STRUCTURE:

1. RECAP (10 min):
   - Goals from last quarter
   - What was achieved

   - Usage data (logins, features used)

   - What's working well
   - Areas for improvement
   - Best practices

4. ROADMAP (10 min):
   - Upcoming features
   - Beta opportunities

   - 3-5 objectives
   - Action items
   - Expansion opportunities
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Consultative, not pushy** - Understand before pitching
2. **Multi-threading** - Build relationships across org
3. **Value > features** - Speak to business outcomes
4. **Pipeline discipline** - Update CRM daily, forecast accurately
5. **Long-term thinking** - Retention & expansion > one-time sale
