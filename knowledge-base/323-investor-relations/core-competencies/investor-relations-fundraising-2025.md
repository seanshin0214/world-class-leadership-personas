## Fundraising Stages

```
PRE-SEED ($500K - $2M):
- Stage: Idea/MVP
- Investors: Angels, micro VCs
- Valuation: $3M - $8M
- Dilution: 15-25%
- Timeline: 2-3 months

SEED ($2M - $5M):
- Stage: Product-market fit
- Metrics: $500K ARR, 50% MoM growth
- Investors: Seed VCs (Sequoia, a16z, etc.)
- Valuation: $10M - $20M
- Dilution: 20-25%
- Timeline: 3-4 months

SERIES A ($10M - $20M):
- Stage: Scaling
- Metrics: $3M ARR, 100% YoY growth, >100% NRR
- Investors: Series A VCs
- Valuation: $30M - $80M
- Dilution: 20-30%
- Timeline: 4-6 months

SERIES B ($25M - $50M):
- Stage: Market leadership
- Metrics: $10M ARR, 80%+ YoY growth, Rule of 40
- Investors: Growth VCs
- Valuation: $100M - $300M
- Dilution: 15-25%
- Timeline: 4-6 months

SERIES C+ ($50M+):
- Stage: Expansion
- Metrics: $50M+ ARR, profitable or path to profitability
- Investors: Late-stage VCs, private equity
- Valuation: $300M+
- Timeline: 4-6 months
```

## The Perfect Pitch Deck

### 15-Slide Structure

```markdown
SLIDE 1: COVER
- Company name & tagline
- One-liner: "We help [target customer] achieve [outcome] by [how]"
- Example: "We help marketing teams increase ROI by 3x with AI-powered analytics"

SLIDE 2: PROBLEM
- What problem are you solving?
- Who has this problem?
- How painful is it? (quantify)
- Current solutions (why they suck)

Example:
"Marketing teams spend 20 hours/week on manual reporting
instead of strategy. Current tools are complex, expensive ($50K/year),
and require data analysts to operate."

SLIDE 3: SOLUTION
- How do you solve it?
- Product demo (screenshots, not just mockups)
- Key features (3-5 main capabilities)
- Unique value proposition

Example:
"Acme Analytics: AI-powered marketing analytics in 3 clicks
- Connect data sources (5 min setup)
- AI generates insights automatically
- Beautiful dashboards (no analyst required)
Result: Save 15 hours/week, increase ROI visibility by 10x"

SLIDE 4: MARKET SIZE
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): $50B
- SAM (Serviceable Available Market): $10B
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): $500M (in 5 years)

Show math:
TAM = 100K companies √ó $50K average spend = $5B
(back it up with data: Gartner, CB Insights, etc.)

SLIDE 5: PRODUCT
- Product screenshots
- Key features
- Product roadmap (next 12 months)
- Technical architecture (if relevant)

SLIDE 6: TRACTION
- Revenue: $5M ARR, growing 15% MoM
- Customers: 150 paying customers
- Growth metrics:
  * Month 1: $100K ARR
  * Month 6: $1M ARR
  * Month 12: $3M ARR
  * Month 18: $5M ARR
- Customer logos (if impressive)

SLIDE 7: BUSINESS MODEL
- Pricing: $500/month (Starter), $2K/month (Pro), $10K/month (Enterprise)
- Unit economics:
  * CAC: $5,000
  * LTV: $30,000
  * LTV/CAC: 6x
  * Payback: 8 months
  * Gross margin: 80%

SLIDE 8: GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY
- Customer acquisition:
  * 40% Inbound (content, SEO)
  * 30% Outbound (sales team)
  * 20% Partnerships
  * 10% Events
- Sales process: 30-day sales cycle, $15K average deal size
- Distribution channels

SLIDE 9: COMPETITION
- Competitive landscape matrix
  |               | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
  |---------------|-----|--------------|--------------|
  | Price         | $$  | $$$$$        | $            |
  | Ease of use   | ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê | ‚≠ê‚≠ê           | ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê         |
  | AI-powered    | ‚úÖ  | ‚ùå           | ‚ùå           |

- Unfair advantage: Proprietary AI models, 2 years ahead

SLIDE 10: WHY NOW?
- Market timing
- Technology enablers (AI, LLMs, cheaper compute)
- Regulatory changes
- Behavior shifts (remote work ‚Üí more SaaS spending)

SLIDE 11: TEAM
- Founder profiles (photos, 1-2 sentence bios)
- Relevant experience
- Why this team can win?
- Example:
  * CEO: 10 years at Google (led $100M product)
  * CTO: PhD in ML from Stanford
  * VP Sales: Scaled sales at Salesforce $0 ‚Üí $50M

SLIDE 12: FINANCIALS
- Historical (last 12 months actuals)
- Forecast (next 3 years)
  |           | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 | 2027 |
  |-----------|------|------|------|------|
  | Revenue   | $5M  | $15M | $40M | $80M |

- How will you spend $20M Series A?
  * 40% Sales & Marketing ($8M)
  * 35% Engineering ($7M)
  * 15% Customer Success ($3M)
  * 10% G&A ($2M)
- Key hires: 20 sales reps, 15 engineers, 8 CSMs
- Expected outcomes: $5M ‚Üí $40M ARR in 24 months

SLIDE 14: ASK
- Raising: $20M Series A
- Valuation: $100M pre-money ($120M post)
- Use: Scale sales, product development
- Milestones: $40M ARR, Rule of 40, profitability path

SLIDE 15: THANK YOU / Q&A
- Contact information
- Website, demo link
- Backup slides (detailed financials, customer testimonials, etc.)
```

### Investor Meeting Prep

```markdown
BEFORE THE MEETING:

1. RESEARCH THE VC
   - Portfolio companies (look for similar stage/industry)
   - Recent investments (are they active?)
   - Partner background (read their blog, Twitter)
   - Typical check size & ownership target

2. PREPARE MATERIALS
   - Pitch deck (PDF + live demo backup)
   - One-pager (elevator pitch, metrics, ask)
   - Financial model (Excel)
   - Customer references (3-5 willing to speak)

3. ANTICIPATE QUESTIONS
   - "Why now?"
   - "How do you compete with [Big Company]?"
   - "What if [risk X] happens?"
   - "Why are you the right team?"
   - "What keeps you up at night?"

DURING THE MEETING:

1. OPENING (5 min)
   - Personal connection (find common ground)
   - Set agenda: "I'll walk through our deck (30 min), then Q&A"

2. PITCH (30 min)
   - Start with traction (hook them early)
   - Tell a story (customer pain ‚Üí solution ‚Üí results)
   - Use data (metrics, growth charts)
   - Be concise (don't read slides verbatim)

3. Q&A (20 min)
   - Listen carefully (understand underlying concern)
   - Be honest (don't BS if you don't know something)
   - Redirect if needed ("Great question, let me get back to you")

4. CLOSING (5 min)
   - Ask for feedback
   - Next steps: "What would you need to see to move forward?"
   - Timeline: "We're closing in 4 weeks, can you commit by [date]?"

AFTER THE MEETING:

1. SEND FOLLOW-UP (Same day)
   - Thank you email
   - Deck + materials
   - Answer any outstanding questions
   - Next steps

2. PROVIDE REFERENCES
   - Intro them to customers
   - Provide data room access (if requested)

3. MAINTAIN MOMENTUM
   - Weekly updates (even if no news)
   - Create FOMO (other investors interested)
```

### Common Due Diligence Questions

```markdown
BUSINESS MODEL:
Q: "What's your customer acquisition strategy?"
A: "40% inbound (content marketing, SEO, $50K/month spend, 300 MQLs),
   30% outbound (5 SDRs, 10 AEs), 20% partnerships, 10% events."

Q: "How do you calculate LTV?"
A: "LTV = ARPU √ó Gross Margin / Churn Rate
   = $500/month √ó 80% / 3% monthly churn
   = $13,333"

COMPETITION:
Q: "What if Google/Microsoft builds this?"
A: "They could, but:
   1. We're 2-3 years ahead (proprietary ML models)
   2. We're focused on SMB/mid-market (not their focus)
   3. Our NPS is 72 (competitors <40)
   We'd welcome being acquired by them!"

TEAM:
Q: "What happens if your CTO leaves?"
A: "We have:
   1. Strong #2 engineer (10 years experience)
   2. Vesting schedule (4-year with 1-year cliff)
   3. Bus factor of 3+ on all critical systems"

RISKS:
Q: "What keeps you up at night?"
A: "Three things:
   1. Competition (we're adding features faster)
   2. Hiring (we need to 3x team in 12 months)
   3. Scaling infrastructure (we're planning ahead)"

Don't hide weaknesses - address them with mitigation plans!
```

## Term Sheet Negotiation

### Key Terms

```markdown
VALUATION:
- Pre-money: $100M
- Investment: $20M
- Post-money: $120M
- Price per share: $10.00
- Fully diluted shares: 12M

‚Üí Investor owns: $20M / $120M = 16.7%

LIQUIDATION PREFERENCE:
Standard: 1x non-participating
- Investor gets $20M back first, then pro-rata
- Example exit: $80M ‚Üí Investor gets $20M + 16.7% √ó $60M = $30M

Avoid: 2x participating
- Investor gets $40M first, then 16.7% √ó $40M = $46.7M
- Founder gets less!

ANTI-DILUTION:
Weighted-average (standard)
- Protects investor if you raise a down round
- Formula adjusts price per share

Avoid: Full ratchet
- If you raise at $5/share (down from $10), investor's price resets to $5
- Heavily punishes founders

BOARD COMPOSITION:
Ideal: 5 seats
- 2 Founders
- 2 Investors
- 1 Independent
‚Üí No one has control

Avoid: Investor control (3 investor seats out of 5)

PRO-RATA RIGHTS:
- Investor can maintain ownership % in future rounds
- Standard for Series A+

VESTING:
Standard: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- Founder gets 25% after 1 year, then monthly
- Protects company if founder leaves early

DRAG-ALONG:
- If majority shareholders approve sale, minority must follow
- Standard (prevents holdouts from blocking exits)

NO-SHOP:
- 30-45 days (you can't talk to other investors)
- Reasonable: 30-45 days
- Red flag: 90+ days
```

### Negotiation Strategy

```markdown
BEFORE NEGOTIATION:
1. Get multiple term sheets (creates leverage)
2. Understand market terms (ask lawyers, other founders)
3. Prioritize what matters (valuation vs control vs investor quality)

DURING NEGOTIATION:
1. Don't negotiate via email (phone or in-person)
2. Focus on win-win (adversarial = bad relationship)
3. Get advice (lawyer, advisors, other founders)

WHAT TO NEGOTIATE:
‚úÖ Valuation (if you have multiple offers)
‚úÖ Liquidation preference (1x non-participating is standard)
‚úÖ Board seats (maintain founder control if possible)
‚úÖ Option pool (increase after, not before, investment)

‚ùå Don't be greedy (bad reputation spreads)
‚ùå Don't optimize for valuation alone (wrong investor = death)

RED FLAGS:
üö© No pro-rata rights offered (investor doesn't believe in you)
üö© Full ratchet anti-dilution (punitive)
üö© Investor control (3+ board seats)
üö© Participating preferred (double-dipping)
üö© Long no-shop (60+ days)
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Fundraise when you don't need to** - Leverage is everything
2. **Investor quality > valuation** - Smart money helps you win
3. **Know your metrics cold** - LTV, CAC, burn, runway, growth rate
4. **Tell a story with data** - Numbers + narrative = compelling
5. **Build relationships early** - VCs invest in people they know
