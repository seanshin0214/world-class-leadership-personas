# Strategic Planning Frameworks 2025

**Updated**: 2025-11-23 | **Focus**: SWOT, PESTEL, Scenario Planning

---

## Core Frameworks

### 1. SWOT Analysis

```markdown
STRENGTHS (Internal)
- Strong brand recognition
- Proprietary technology
- Skilled team

WEAKNESSES (Internal)
- Limited cash flow
- Narrow product line
- High employee turnover

OPPORTUNITIES (External)
- Growing market demand
- New technology trends
- Strategic partnerships

THREATS (External)
- Increased competition
- Regulatory changes
- Economic downturn
```

**When to Use**: Initial strategy assessment, quarterly reviews

### 2. PESTEL Analysis

```
POLITICAL
- Government stability
- Tax policies
- Trade regulations

ECONOMIC
- GDP growth
- Interest rates
- Inflation

SOCIAL
- Demographics
- Lifestyle trends
- Education levels

TECHNOLOGICAL
- Innovation rate
- Automation
- R&D investment

ENVIRONMENTAL
- Climate change
- Sustainability
- Resource scarcity

LEGAL
- Employment law
- Consumer protection
- IP regulations
```

### 3. Porter's Five Forces

```
THREAT OF NEW ENTRANTS
↓
BARGAINING POWER ← COMPETITIVE ← BARGAINING POWER
OF SUPPLIERS        RIVALRY      OF BUYERS
                      ↑
              THREAT OF SUBSTITUTES
```

**Analysis**:
- High barriers to entry = Favorable
- Many substitutes = Unfavorable
- Strong buyer power = Unfavorable

## Scenario Planning

### 2x2 Matrix Method

```
         HIGH UNCERTAINTY
              │
    SCENARIO A│SCENARIO B
    Optimistic│Pessimistic
   High Growth│Low Growth
──────────────┼──────────────
    SCENARIO C│SCENARIO D
    Tech Boom │Disruption
   Stable Regs│Strict Regs
              │
         LOW UNCERTAINTY
```

**Steps**:
1. Identify key uncertainties (2)
2. Create 4 scenarios
3. Develop strategies for each
4. Monitor indicators
5. Adapt plan

### Scenario Narratives

**Scenario A (Best Case)**:
- Market grows 30% YoY
- Low competition
- Strong partnerships
- Strategy: Aggressive expansion

**Scenario D (Worst Case)**:
- Market shrinks 10%
- New regulations
- Supply chain issues
- Strategy: Cost reduction, focus core

## Ansoff Matrix (Growth Strategies)

```
            Existing Products │ New Products
────────────┼───────────────┼────────────────
Existing    │ PENETRATION   │ DEVELOPMENT
Markets     │ (Low Risk)    │ (Medium Risk)
────────────┼───────────────┼────────────────
New         │ EXPANSION     │ DIVERSIFICATION
Markets     │ (Medium Risk) │ (High Risk)
```

**Examples**:
- Penetration: Increase marketing to existing customers
- Development: Launch new features
- Expansion: Enter new geography
- Diversification: Enter new industry

## Strategic Planning Process

### Phase 1: Assessment (Weeks 1-2)

```markdown
□ SWOT analysis
□ PESTEL scan
□ Competitive analysis
□ Financial review
□ Stakeholder interviews
```

### Phase 2: Strategy Formulation (Weeks 3-4)

```markdown
□ Define vision & mission
□ Set 3-year goals
□ Identify strategic initiatives
□ Scenario planning
□ Risk assessment
```

### Phase 3: Execution (Weeks 5-6)

```markdown
□ Develop OKRs
□ Assign owners
□ Resource allocation
□ Communication plan
□ Quick wins identification
```

### Phase 4: Monitoring (Ongoing)

```markdown
□ Monthly KPI review
□ Quarterly OKR check-ins
□ Annual strategy refresh
□ Continuous feedback
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Multiple frameworks**: Use 2-3 together for complete picture
2. **Quantify**: Add numbers to qualitative analysis
3. **Scenarios**: Prepare for multiple futures
4. **Review cycle**: Quarterly minimum
5. **Stakeholder buy-in**: Involve early and often
