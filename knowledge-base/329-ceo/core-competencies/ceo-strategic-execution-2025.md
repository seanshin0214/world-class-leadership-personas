## Strategic Planning Framework

### The 3 Horizons Model (McKinsey)

```
HORIZON 1: Defend & Extend Core Business (70% of resources)
- Optimize existing products/services
- Incremental innovation
- Efficiency improvements
- Current revenue engine

HORIZON 2: Build Emerging Businesses (20% of resources)
- New products in adjacentmarkets
- New customer segments
- New business models
- Future revenue (1-3 years)

HORIZON 3: Create Transformational Opportunities (10% of resources)
- Moonshots, R&D
- Disruptive innovation
- Long-term bets
- Options on future (3-10 years)

BALANCE IS CRITICAL:
- Too much H1: Optimize yourself to irrelevance
- Too much H3: Burn cash on dreams
- Right mix: Grow today while building tomorrow
```

### Annual Strategic Planning Process

```markdown
PHASE 1: SITUATION ANALYSIS (January)
- Review last year's performance (vs goals)
- Market analysis (size, growth, trends)
- Competitive landscape (who's winning, why)
- Customer feedback (NPS, churn analysis, surveys)
- Internal capabilities (strengths, weaknesses)

PHASE 2: STRATEGIC CHOICES (February)
- Where to play? (Markets, segments, geographies)
- How to win? (Differentiation, cost leadership, niche)
- What to stop? (Products, markets, initiatives to kill)
- Resource allocation (budget, people, time)

PHASE 3: GOAL SETTING (March)
Company-level OKRs:
Objective: Become the #1 platform for [X]
- KR1: Reach $50M ARR (from $25M)
- KR2: NRR >120%
- KR3: Launch in 3 new markets
- KR4: Team NPS >60

Cascade to departments:
- Engineering: Ship 5 major features, 99.9% uptime
- Sales: $30M new ARR, CAC <$10K
- Marketing: 10K MQLs, 50% conversion
- Customer Success: <5% churn, 115% NRR

PHASE 4: QUARTERLY EXECUTION (Q1-Q4)
- Monthly business reviews (actual vs plan)
- Weekly leadership team sync
- Quarterly all-hands (celebrate wins, course-correct)

PHASE 5: ANNUAL REVIEW (December)
- Score OKRs (0-1.0 scale)
- Celebrate achievements
- Document lessons learned
- Feed into next year's planning
```

## Decision-Making Framework

### The Regret Minimization Framework (Jeff Bezos)

```python
def should_i_do_this(decision):
    """
    Project yourself to age 80, look back on your life.
    Will you regret NOT doing this?
    """
    
    # Type 1 Decisions: One-way doors (hard to reverse)
    # - Hiring a C-level executive
    # - Acquiring a company
    # - Pivoting the business
    # â†’ Slow, careful, reversible
    
    type1_examples = [
        "Should we pivot to B2B from B2C?",
        "Should we acquire CompetitorX for $50M?",
        "Should we raise $100M Series C?"
    ]
    
    # Type 2 Decisions: Two-way doors (reversible)
    # - Launching a new feature
    # - Changing pricing (can revert)
    # - Running a marketing campaign
    # â†’ Fast, experiment, learn
    
    type2_examples = [
        "Should we test $99/month pricing?",
        "Should we try LinkedIn ads?",
        "Should we launch feature X?"
    ]
    
    if decision in type1_examples:
        approach = "Slow & deliberate"
        team_involvement = "High (get input from many)"
        reversibility = "Low (hard to undo)"
        decision_speed = "Days to weeks"
    else:
        approach = "Fast & experimental"
        team_involvement = "Low (empower team to decide)"
        reversibility = "High (easy to undo)"
        decision_speed = "Hours to days"
    
    return {
        'approach': approach,
        'involvement': team_involvement,
        'reversibility': reversibility,
        'speed': decision_speed
    }

# Example usage
decision = "Should we acquire CompetitorX for $50M?"
framework = should_i_do_this(decision)

# Output:
# {
#     'approach': 'Slow & deliberate',
#     'involvement': 'High (get input from many)',
#     'reversibility': 'Low (hard to undo)',
#     'decision_speed': 'Days to weeks'
# }
```

### DACI Decision Framework

```
DRIVER: Owns decision, drives process
APPROVER: Final say (usually CEO for major decisions)
CONTRIBUTORS: Provide input, expertise
INFORMED: Notified of decision

Example: Should we open London office?

DRIVER: VP International (gathers data, makes recommendation)
APPROVER: CEO (final yes/no)
CONTRIBUTORS:
- CFO (financial analysis)
- VP Sales (market opportunity)
- VP Operations (hiring, legal, logistics)
- VP Product (localization requirements)

INFORMED:
- All employees (via announcement)
- Board (at next meeting)

TIMELINE:
Week 1-2: Driver gathers data from Contributors
Week 3: Driver presents recommendation to Approver
Week 4: Approver makes decision
Week 4+: Informed parties notified
```

## People Management (The #1 CEO Job)

### Hiring A-Players

```
A-PLAYER DEFINITION:
- Top 10% in their role
- Self-motivated, low maintenance
- Raises the bar for everyone
- 10x more productive than B-player

COST OF MIS-HIRE (C-LEVEL):
- Direct: $500K+ (salary, severance, replacement cost)
- Indirect: Lost opportunities, team morale, customer churn
- Total: $2-5M over 2 years

HIRING PROCESS (C-LEVEL):

STEP 1: Define Success Profile
- What does success look like in 12 months?
- Must-have vs nice-to-have skills
- Culture fit (values alignment)

STEP 2: Sourcing (100 candidates â†’ 10 interviews â†’ 1 offer)
- Headhunters ($50-100K fee, but worth it)
- Network referrals (best source)
- Passive candidates (not actively looking)

STEP 3: Structured Interviews
Interview 1 (60 min): Career History
- Walk through resume chronologically
- Dig deep into each role (achievements, failures, why left)
- Pattern recognition (trajectory, growth mindset)

Interview 2 (90 min): Case Study
- Real problem from your company
- "How would you approach [challenge]?"
- Evaluate thinking process, not perfect answer

Interview 3 (60 min): Team Fit
- Meet potential direct reports
- Meet peers (other VPs)
- Assess collaboration style

Interview 4 (45 min): Values & Culture
- Describe a time you had to make a hard decision
- How do you give/receive feedback?
- What's your management philosophy?

STEP 4: Reference Checks (CRITICAL!)
- 5-7 references (not just the ones they give you)
- Back-channel references (LinkedIn connections)
- Questions:
  * "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate this person?"
  * "Would you hire them again?"
  * "What's their superpower? What's their kryptonite?"

STEP 5: Offer (Close Fast!)
- Competitive compensation (don't cheap out on A-players)
- Sell the vision (why this is the opportunity of their career)
- Address concerns (spouse, relocation, risk)
```

### Performance Management

```markdown

- What's on track?
- What's blocked?
- How can I help?

Rating Scale:
- Exceeds (1.0+): 130% of goal, promotion-worthy
- Meets (0.7-1.0): On track, good performance
- Partially Meets (0.4-0.6): Below target, PIP
- Does Not Meet (<0.4): Termination

4. CALIBRATE (Forced Ranking)
- Top 20%: Exceeds (promote, raise, bonus)
- Middle 70%: Meets (standard raise/bonus)
- Bottom 10%: Partially/Does Not Meet (PIP or exit)

5. COMPENSATION ADJUSTMENTS
- Top performers: 15-25% raise + equity refresh
- Solid performers: 5-10% raise
- Underperformers: 0% raise + PIP

ANNUAL BONUS POOL:
- Company performance: 50% weight
- Individual performance: 50% weight

Example:
- Company hits 90% of goal â†’ 90% of bonus pool
- Individual exceeds (1.2x) â†’ Gets 1.2x their target bonus
- Net: 90% Ã— 1.2 = 108% of target bonus
```

## Crisis Management

### Playbook for Major Incident

```markdown
SCENARIO: Major security breach, customer data compromised

HOUR 1: ASSESS & CONTAIN
- Assemble crisis team (CEO, CTO, CISO, Legal, Comms)
- Confirm scope (how many customers affected?)
- Immediate containment (shut down vulnerable systems)
- Preserve evidence (logs, forensics)

HOUR 2-6: INTERNAL COMMUNICATION
- Notify board chair (ASAP)
- All-hands meeting (be transparent, calm team fears)
- War room setup (24/7 operations until resolved)

HOUR 6-24: EXTERNAL COMMUNICATION
- Notify affected customers (email, phone for large accounts)
- Public statement (website, blog, social media)
- Regulatory notification (GDPR, SOC 2 auditors)
- Media response (prepared statement, no interviews yet)

Example Customer Email:
---
Subject: Important Security Update

Dear [Customer Name],

I'm writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your account.

WHAT HAPPENED:
On [Date], we discovered unauthorized access to our database. We immediately shut down the affected systems and engaged a leading cybersecurity firm to investigate.

WHAT DATA WAS AFFECTED:
- Email addresses and names
- Account activity logs
- NOT affected: Passwords (encrypted), payment info (not stored)

WHAT WE'RE DOING:
- Implementing additional security measures
- Offering free credit monitoring for 1 year
- Daily updates until resolved

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO:
- Change your password (as a precaution)
- Enable 2FA (we'll guide you)
- Watch for suspicious emails

We take this extremely seriously and apologize for any concern this causes.

For questions: security@company.com or call [phone]

Sincerely,
[CEO Name], CEO
---

DAY 2-7: REMEDIATION
- Root cause analysis (how did this happen?)
- Fixes implemented (patch vulnerabilities)
- Enhanced monitoring (prevent recurrence)
- Regular updates to customers, board, public

DAY 7-30: RECOVERY
- Post-mortem report (detailed timeline, lessons learned)
- Policy updates (security practices, incident response)
- Team training (prevent future incidents)
- Customer trust rebuilding (transparency, over-communication)

LONG-TERM:
- External audit (validate security posture)
- Insurance claim (cyber insurance payout)
- Process improvements (make lemonade from lemons)
```

3. **Metrics obsession** - What gets measured gets managed
