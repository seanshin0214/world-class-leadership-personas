# School Counseling & Student Support 2025

**Updated**: 2025-11-23 | **Focus**: ASCA Model, Crisis Intervention, College Counseling

## ASCA National Model

```markdown
COMPREHENSIVE SCHOOL COUNSELING PROGRAM:

FOUR COMPONENTS:

1. FOUNDATION:
   - Mission & vision (aligned with school goals)
   - Beliefs (all students can succeed)
   - Student competencies (mindsets & behaviors)

2. MANAGEMENT:
   - Annual calendar (when activities happen)
   - Data analysis (achievement gaps, attendance)
   - Advisory council (stakeholders: admin, teachers, parents)

3. DELIVERY:
   - Direct services (80% of time)
     * Individual counseling
     * Small group counseling
     * Classroom lessons
   - Indirect services (20% of time)
     * Consultation with teachers, parents
     * Referrals to community resources

4. ACCOUNTABILITY:
   - Data collection (pre/post surveys, grades, behavior)
   - Program evaluation (did we make a difference?)
   - Annual report (share results with stakeholders)

---

TIME ALLOCATION (ASCA recommended):

DIRECT STUDENT SERVICES (80%):
- 30-40%: Individual/small group counseling
- 25-35%: Classroom lessons
- 10-15%: Other student interactions

INDIRECT SERVICES (20%):
- Consultation, collaboration
- Referrals

NON-COUNSELING TASKS (minimize):
❌ Bus duty
❌ Lunch supervision
❌ Testing coordinator (should be separate role)
✅ Some involvement ok, but not primary duty

---

STUDENT COMPETENCIES:

MINDSETS:
- M1: Self-confidence in ability to succeed
- M2: Sense of belonging
- M3: Understanding effort leads to success
- M4: Understanding academic skills important
- M5: Belief in development of whole self
- M6: Positive attitude toward work & learning

BEHAVIORS:
- Learning strategies (goal-setting, time management)
- Self-management (impulse control, perseverance)
- Social skills (communication, teamwork, empathy)
```

## Individual Counseling

```markdown
APPROACH: Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)

PRINCIPLES:
- Focus on solutions (not problems)
- Build on strengths (not deficits)
- Brief (3-6 sessions typical)
- Goal-oriented

SESSION STRUCTURE:

1. BUILD RAPPORT (5 min):
   - "How was your day?"
   - Small talk (interests, hobbies)
   - Trust is foundation

2. IDENTIFY PROBLEM (5 min):
   - "What brought you here today?"
   - Active listening (reflect feelings)
   - "It sounds like you're feeling frustrated about..."

3. MIRACLE QUESTION (5 min):
   - "Imagine you go to sleep tonight, and a miracle happens. When you wake up, the problem is solved. What's different?"
   - Client: "I'd have friends to sit with at lunch"
   - Counselor: "What else?"
   - Client: "I'd feel less lonely"

4. SCALING QUESTION (5 min):
   - "On a scale of 1-10, where are you now?"
   - Client: "3"
   - "What would it take to get to a 4?"
   - Small steps (not 3 to 10 overnight)

5. EXCEPTION QUESTIONS (5 min):
   - "When was a time the problem wasn't as bad?"
   - "What was different then?"
   - Identify what works

6. GOAL SETTING (5 min):
   - SMART goal
   - Example: "I will sit with someone new at lunch 2× this week"
   - Concrete, achievable

7. HOMEWORK (5 min):
   - Action steps
   - "Try sitting with someone new. We'll check in next week"

8. FOLLOW-UP:
   - Next session: "How did it go?"
   - Adjust plan as needed
   - Celebrate progress

---

COMMON ISSUES:

ANXIETY:
- Symptoms: Worry, avoidance, physical (headache, stomachache)
- Interventions:
  * Deep breathing (4-7-8 technique)
  * Progressive muscle relaxation
  * Thought challenging (is it realistic?)
  * Graded exposure (start small, build up)
- Refer if: Panic attacks, severe impairment

DEPRESSION:
- Symptoms: Sadness, hopelessness, loss of interest, fatigue
- Interventions:
  * Behavioral activation (schedule pleasant activities)
  * Social connection (reach out to friends)
  * Sleep hygiene
  * Exercise
- Refer if: Suicidal ideation, self-harm, severe impairment

GRIEF:
- Triggers: Death of family member, pet, divorce
- Interventions:
  * Normalize feelings (it's ok to cry, be angry)
  * Memorialize (draw picture, write letter)
  * Maintain routines (school provides structure)
  * Connect with support (family, friends, counselor)
- Refer if: Complicated grief (not improving after 6 months)

---

CONFIDENTIALITY:

WHEN TO BREAK:
- Harm to self (suicide, self-harm)
- Harm to others (violence, abuse)
- Abuse or neglect (mandatory reporter)

EXPLAIN TO STUDENTS:
"What we talk about is private, but if you tell me you're going to hurt yourself or someone else, or someone is hurting you, I have to tell someone to keep you safe"

DOCUMENTATION:
- Keep brief notes (date, topic, intervention)
- Store securely (locked file cabinet)
- Parents can request records (be mindful of what you write)
```

## Group Counseling

```markdown
TYPES:

SKILLS-BASED:
- Social skills
- Study skills
- Anger management
- Friendship skills

SUPPORT:
- New students
- Divorced/separated families
- Grief & loss

---

GROUP STRUCTURE (8-week example):

WEEK 1: Introduction
- Ground rules (confidentiality, respect, one person talks)
- Icebreaker (name, favorite hobby)
- Overview (what we'll do, why)

WEEK 2-7: Content
- Activity (e.g., role play social skill)
- Discussion (how does this apply?)
- Practice (try this week)
- Check-in (how did homework go?)

WEEK 8: Closure
- Review what we learned
- Celebrate progress
- Graduation certificates

---

SIZE & FREQUENCY:
- Elementary: 3-4 students, 30 min, weekly
- Middle/high: 5-8 students, 40-50 min, weekly

SELECTION:
- Teacher referral
- Self-referral
- Counselor observation
- Data (low grades, absences, discipline)

CONSENT:
- Parent permission required
- Student assent (want to participate)
```

## Classroom Lessons

```markdown
DOMAINS (ASCA):

ACADEMIC:
- Study skills (note-taking, test-taking)
- Growth mindset (effort matters)
- Goal-setting (SMART goals)
- Organization (planners, binders)

SOCIAL-EMOTIONAL:
- Feelings identification (emotion wheel)
- Empathy (perspective-taking)
- Conflict resolution (I-statements, compromise)
- Bullying prevention (upstander vs bystander)

COLLEGE & CAREER:
- Career exploration (interest inventories)
- College readiness (SAT, financial aid)
- Resume writing
- Interview skills

---

LESSON PLAN (30 min):

1. HOOK (5 min):
   - Engaging activity (video, story, question)
   - "What does success mean to you?"

2. DIRECT INSTRUCTION (10 min):
   - Teach concept
   - Examples

3. ACTIVITY (10 min):
   - Practice (worksheet, role play, discussion)
   - Example: SMART goal worksheet

4. DEBRIEF (5 min):
   - "What did you learn?"
   - "How will you use this?"
   - Preview next lesson

---

CLASSROOM MANAGEMENT:
- Establish expectations (same as teacher's rules)
- Attention signal (raise hand, countdown)
- Praise positive behavior
- Redirect off-task behavior (proximity, cue)
- Partner with teacher (they set tone)
```

## Crisis Intervention

```markdown
CRISIS TYPES:

SUICIDE RISK:
- Warning signs:
  * Talking about death, dying, "I wish I wasn't here"
  * Giving away possessions
  * Withdrawal from friends, activities
  * Changes in mood, behavior
  * Risky behavior (substance use, reckless driving)

RESPONSE:
1. Ask directly: "Are you thinking about suicide?"
   - Won't "plant idea" (myth!)
   - Shows you care
2. Assess: 
   - Plan? (specific = higher risk)
   - Means? (access to gun, pills = higher risk)
   - Timeline? (tonight = immediate risk)
3. Safety plan:
   - Remove means (give pills to parent)
   - Identify supports (who to call)
   - Crisis line: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
4. Do NOT leave alone
5. Contact parent immediately
6. Follow up (next day, weekly)

---

SELF-HARM:
- Cutting, burning, hitting self
- NOT suicide attempt (usually coping mechanism)
- Response:
  * Don't judge ("Why would you do that?")
  * Understand function (release emotion, feel control)
  * Alternative coping (ice cube, snap rubber band, journal)
  * Safety plan (if urge, call counselor/parent)
  * Refer to therapist

---

SCHOOL VIOLENCE THREAT:
- "I'm going to shoot up the school"
- TAKE ALL THREATS SERIOUSLY
- Response:
  1. Ensure safety (remove student, lock down if needed)
  2. Notify admin immediately
  3. Threat assessment (multidisciplinary team)
  4. Contact parent, possibly police
  5. Follow-up plan (counseling, safety plan)

---

TRAUMA (witnessing violence, abuse):
- Symptoms: Hypervigilance, nightmares, flashbacks, avoidance
- Response:
  * Safety first (is student safe now?)
  * Report abuse (mandatory reporter)
  * Provide support (predictability, routines)
  * Refer to trauma therapist (EMDR, TF-CBT)
  * Educate teachers (trauma-informed practices)
```

## College & Career Counseling

```markdown
MIDDLE SCHOOL:

CAREER EXPLORATION:
- Interest inventories (Strong Interest Inventory, O*NET)
- Career day (guest speakers)
- Job shadows
- Career clusters (16 broad categories: healthcare, STEM, arts, etc.)

COURSE PLANNING:
- 4-year plan (what classes to take)
- Explain sequences (Algebra I → Geometry → Algebra II)
- Electives (explore interests)

---

HIGH SCHOOL:

COLLEGE PLANNING:

FRESHMAN:
- GPA matters (cumulative from 9th grade)
- Get involved (clubs, sports, volunteering)
- Start college savings (529 plan)

SOPHOMORE:
- PSAT/PreACT (practice)
- Continue activities (depth > breadth)
- Start college visits (if possible)

JUNIOR (busiest year!):
- SAT/ACT (take 2-3 times, superscore)
- AP exams (college credit)
- College fairs, visits
- Narrow list (reach, match, safety schools)

SENIOR:
- Applications (Common App, Coalition App)
  * Fall deadline: Nov 1 (early action/decision)
  * Regular: Jan 1
- Essays (personal statement, supplemental)
- Letters of recommendation (ask teachers early, Sept)
- FAFSA (Oct 1 opens, Jan 1 priority deadline)
- CSS Profile (for institutional aid)
- Financial aid packages (compare, appeal if needed)
- Decision: May 1 (National Decision Day)

---

APPLICATION COMPONENTS:

PERSONAL STATEMENT:
- 650 words (Common App)
- Prompts: Significant experience, challenge faced, topic of your choice
- Tips:
  * Show, don't tell (use specific examples)
  * Be authentic (not what you think they want to hear)
  * Multiple drafts (get feedback)

RESUME:
- Academics (GPA, test scores, AP courses)
- Activities (leadership roles, years involved)
- Work experience (jobs, internships)
- Volunteer (hours, impact)
- Awards/honors

LETTERS OF RECOMMENDATION:
- 2-3 letters (teacher, counselor, optional: coach/mentor)
- Ask teachers who know you well (junior year teachers)
- Provide "brag sheet" (resume, accomplishments)
- Waive right to read (shows integrity)

---

FINANCIAL AID:

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid):
- EFC (Expected Family Contribution)
- Pell Grant (up to $7,395, no repayment)
- Subsidized loans (gov't pays interest while in school)
- Unsubsidized loans (interest accrues)

SCHOLARSHIPS:
- Merit (grades, test scores)
- Need-based (family income)
- Outside scholarships (local businesses, orgs)
- Apply early, apply often

NET PRICE CALCULATOR:
- On college websites
- Estimate actual cost after aid
- $70K sticker price → $30K net price (with aid)

---

ALTERNATIVES TO 4-YEAR COLLEGE:

COMMUNITY COLLEGE:
- 2 years, then transfer
- Save $40K-$60K
- Same degree (BA/BS)

TRADE SCHOOL:
- Electrician, plumber, HVAC (6 months-2 years)
- High demand, good pay ($50K-$80K)
- Less debt

GAP YEAR:
- Travel, work, volunteer
- Clarify goals
- Apply to college, defer admission 1 year

MILITARY:
- GI Bill (pays for college)
- Job training
- Discipline, structure
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Relationship-first** - Trust is foundation for all counseling
2. **Solution-focused** - Build on strengths, not just problems
3. **Data-driven** - Track outcomes, show impact
4. **Collaboration** - Work with teachers, parents, community
5. **Self-care** - Secondary trauma is real (get supervision, therapy)
