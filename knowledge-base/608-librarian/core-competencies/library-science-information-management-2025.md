## Reference Services

```markdown
REFERENCE INTERVIEW:

STEPS:

1. OPEN QUESTION:
   - "How can I help you today?"
   - Let patron explain need

2. CLARIFY:
   - Paraphrase: "So you're looking for books about..."
   - Ask follow-up: "Is this for school, work, or personal interest?"

3. NARROW SCOPE:
   - "What age level?" (children, teen, adult)
   - "What format?" (book, ebook, audiobook, article)
   - "How much detail?" (overview vs comprehensive)

4. SEARCH TOGETHER:
   - Show process (teach patron to fish)
   - "Let's start with the catalog..."
   - Demonstrate database search

5. FOLLOW-UP:
   - "Did you find what you need?"
   - "Would you like help with anything else?"
   - Give contact info (return if questions)

---

COMMON QUESTIONS:

"WHERE IS THE BATHROOM?"
- Most common question!
- Give clear directions
- Walk patron if needed

"DO YOU HAVE FREE WI-FI?"
- Network name, password (if required)
- Time limit (if applicable)
- Computer use policy

"CAN YOU HELP ME PRINT?"
- Show how to print
- Pricing ($0.10/page black & white, $0.50 color)
- Payment method (cash, card, print credits)

"I NEED BOOKS ABOUT X":
- Use catalog (author, title, subject search)
- Call number (Dewey Decimal or Library of Congress)
- Show shelf location
- Suggest related topics

HOMEWORK HELP (students):
- Don't do homework (teach research skills)
- Show databases (EBSCO, JSTOR, ProQuest)
- Citation help (MLA, APA, Chicago)
- Remind of plagiarism

---

INFORMATION LITERACY:

CRAAP TEST (Evaluate sources):

CURRENCY:
- When published? Updated?
- Is info current or outdated?
- Links work?

RELEVANCE:
- Does it answer your question?
- Appropriate level (not too basic/advanced)?
- Target audience (scholars, general public)?

AUTHORITY:
- Who is author? Credentials?
- Publisher (academic, commercial, self-published)?
- References/bibliography included?

ACCURACY:
- Evidence provided?
- Can you verify info elsewhere?
- Grammar, spelling errors (red flag)?

PURPOSE:
- Why was this created?
- Objective (inform) or biased (persuade, sell)?
- Conflicts of interest?

---

DATABASES & RESOURCES:

ACADEMIC:
- JSTOR (scholarly journals, 12M+ articles)
- EBSCO (academic databases, 375+ databases)
- ProQuest (dissertations, newspapers, journals)
- PubMed (medical, biomedical literature)

GENERAL:
- Google Scholar (free, academic articles)
- Wikipedia (starting point, not citable source)
- News databases (NYT, WSJ, newspapers)

SPECIALIZED:
- Ancestry.com (genealogy)
- Fold3 (military records)
- LexisNexis (legal, news)
- Statista (statistics)

ACCESS:
- Library card (free with card)
- Remote access (login from home)
- Interlibrary loan (request from other libraries)
```

## Collection Development

```markdown
SELECTION CRITERIA:

QUALITY:
- Reviews (Kirkus, Publishers Weekly, School Library Journal)
- Awards (Newbery, Caldecott, Pulitzer)
- Author reputation
- Publisher (reputable vs vanity press)

DEMAND:
- Patron requests (suggestion forms)
- Holds (waitlist = high demand)
- Circulation stats (what's popular?)
- Bestseller lists (NYT, Amazon)

RELEVANCE:
- Community needs (demographics, interests)
- Curriculum support (for school libraries)
- Gaps in collection (underrepresented topics)

BUDGET:
- Limited funds (prioritize)
- Price per circulation (cost-effective)
- Format (hardcover > paperback durability, ebook no wear)

DIVERSITY:
- #OwnVoices (authors from group they write about)
- Representation (race, gender, disability, LGBTQ+)
- Multiple perspectives (not just Western, male, white)

---

WEEDING (Removing books):

MUSTIE:

M - MISLEADING (outdated info)
- Medical (new treatments)
- Technology (10-year-old computer book)
- Travel (hotel closed, prices changed)

U - UGLY (worn, damaged)
- Torn pages
- Missing covers
- Mold, water damage

S - SUPERSEDED (newer edition exists)
- Encyclopedia (2005 vs 2025)
- Textbooks

T - TRIVIAL (no lasting value)
- Fad books (Pet Rock care)
- Celebrity gossip

I - IRRELEVANT (to community)
- No longer circulates
- Doesn't match patron needs

E - ELSEWHERE (available in other format)
- Classic (many copies, only need 1-2)
- Available as ebook

GUIDELINES:
- Fiction: 5 years no circulation → weed
- Nonfiction: 10 years old + not circulating → weed
- Reference: Update annually (almanacs, yearbooks)
- Classics: Keep 1-2 copies (replace if damaged)

---

BUDGETING:

ALLOCATION (example $100K budget):

ADULT FICTION: 25% ($25K)
- Popular fiction, bestsellers
- High circulation

ADULT NONFICTION: 25% ($25K)
- Biography, history, self-help
- Moderate circulation

CHILDREN: 20% ($20K)
- Picture books, easy readers, middle grade
- High circulation (families)

YOUNG ADULT: 10% ($10K)
- Teen fiction, nonfiction
- Lower circulation (smaller demographic)

AUDIOBOOKS: 5% ($5K)
- Downloadable (Overdrive, hoopla)
- Physical CDs (less popular)

EBOOKS: 10% ($10K)
- Simultaneous use titles (expensive but no waitlist)
- Metered access (cheaper, but limited checkouts)

DATABASES: 5% ($5K)
- Annual subscriptions
- High per-use value

REPLACEMENT: 5% ($5K)
- Lost/damaged books

---

VENDORS:

BAKER & TAYLOR:
- Major distributor
- 40% discount (library rate)
- Processing services (labels, covers)

INGRAM:
- Distributor (books, ebooks)
- Fast shipping

MIDWEST TAPE:
- Audiobooks (physical CDs)

OVERDRIVE / LIBBY:
- Ebook/audiobook lending platform
- Pay per copy (perpetual or metered)
- Mobile app (patrons love)

HOOPLA:
- Instant access (no holds)
- Pay per checkout ($1-3 per use)
- Expensive but convenient
```

## Cataloging & Classification

```markdown
MARC RECORD (MAchine-Readable Cataloging):

FIELDS:

020 - ISBN:
020 __ $a 9780143127741 $q (hardcover)

100 - AUTHOR:
100 1_ $a Obama, Michelle, $d 1964-

245 - TITLE:
245 10 $a Becoming / $c Michelle Obama.

250 - EDITION:
250 __ $a First edition.

260 - PUBLICATION:
260 __ $a New York : $b Crown, $c 2018.

300 - PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION:
300 __ $a 448 pages ; $c 25 cm

520 - SUMMARY:
520 __ $a Memoir by former First Lady of the United States.

650 - SUBJECT:
650 _0 $a Presidents' spouses $z United States $v Biography.
650 _0 $a African American women $v Biography.

---

DEWEY DECIMAL CLASSIFICATION (DDC):

000 - COMPUTER SCIENCE, INFORMATION
100 - PHILOSOPHY & PSYCHOLOGY
200 - RELIGION
300 - SOCIAL SCIENCES
400 - LANGUAGE
500 - SCIENCE
600 - TECHNOLOGY (Applied sciences)
700 - ARTS & RECREATION
800 - LITERATURE
900 - HISTORY & GEOGRAPHY

EXAMPLE:
- 641.5 = Cooking
  * 600 = Technology
  * 640 = Home economics
  * 641 = Food & drink
  * 641.5 = Cooking

CALL NUMBER:
641.5 SMI (Dewey + First 3 letters of author's last name)

---

(Used in academic libraries)

A - GENERAL WORKS
B - PHILOSOPHY, PSYCHOLOGY, RELIGION
C-F - HISTORY
G - GEOGRAPHY, ANTHROPOLOGY
H - SOCIAL SCIENCES
J - POLITICAL SCIENCE
K - LAW
L - EDUCATION
M - MUSIC
N - FINE ARTS
P - LANGUAGE & LITERATURE
Q - SCIENCE
R - MEDICINE
S - AGRICULTURE
T - TECHNOLOGY
U - MILITARY SCIENCE
V - NAVAL SCIENCE
Z - BIBLIOGRAPHY, LIBRARY SCIENCE

EXAMPLE:
- PS3568.O243 B43 2018 = "Becoming" by Michelle Obama
  * PS = American literature
  * 3568 = 20th/21st century
  * .O243 = Obama (Cutter number)
  * B43 = Becoming (work)
```

## Circulation & Policies

```markdown
LOAN PERIODS:

BOOKS:
- Adult: 21 days (3 weeks)
- Children: 21 days
- New releases: 7 days (high demand)
- Renewals: 2× (if no holds)

AUDIOBOOKS/DVDs:
- 7 days (shorter, to increase circulation)
- Renewals: 1× (if no holds)

EBOOKS/AUDIOBOOKS (Digital):
- 14-21 days (automatic return, no late fees)
- Simultaneous use (unlimited checkouts) vs metered (26 checkouts then repurchase)

MAGAZINES:
- Latest issue: In-library only
- Back issues: 7 days

---

OVERDUE FINES:

TRADITIONAL:
- $0.25/day (books)
- $1.00/day (DVDs, new releases)
- Max fine: $10 per item
- Replacement cost if 30+ days overdue

FINE-FREE MOVEMENT:
- Growing trend (eliminate barriers)
- Still charge for lost/damaged (replacement cost)
- Research: Fines don't improve returns, hurt low-income patrons
- Libraries adopting: Chicago, San Diego, San Francisco

---

LOST/DAMAGED:

LOST:
- 30 days overdue = Lost status
- Charge replacement cost + $5 processing fee
- If returned later, refund (minus any accrued fines)

DAMAGED:
- Minor (bent pages, small tear): No charge
- Major (large tear, water damage, missing pages): Replacement cost
- Patron can replace (buy same book, bring to library)

---

RESERVES (Holds):

PROCESS:
1. Patron places hold (online catalog, phone, in-person)
2. Item returned → Library pulls from returns
3. Hold shelf (alphabetical by last name)
4. Notify patron (email, text, phone)
5. Hold 7 days (then returned to shelf if not picked up)

PRIORITY:
- First come, first served
- Some libraries: Patrons from same system (consortium) lower priority than home library

DIGITAL:
- Automatic hold (waitlist)
- Auto-delivery when available
- "Recommend for purchase" if long waitlist
```

## Digital Libraries

```markdown
PLATFORMS:

OVERDRIVE / LIBBY:
- Most popular
- Mobile app (iOS, Android)
- Kindle integration
- Audiobooks, ebooks, magazines

HOOPLA:
- No holds (instant access)
- Movies, music, audiobooks, ebooks
- Pay-per-use (library pays per checkout)

KANOPY:
- Streaming video (documentaries, indie films, Great Courses)
- Free for patrons (library pays per view)
- 10 plays/month (some libraries limit)

PRESSREADER:
- Newspapers, magazines (7,000+ titles)
- International (60+ languages)
- Same-day access

---

EBOOK CHALLENGES:

PRICING:
- Ebook often costs MORE than print (library)
- Publisher restrictions (Macmillan: 8-week embargo on new releases)
- Metered access (expires after 26-52 checkouts)

PERPETUAL vs METERED:
- Perpetual: $50-$100 (own forever, but simultaneous use limits)
- Metered (26 checkouts): $20-$40 (then repurchase)
- Cost per circulation: Metered often cheaper if high-demand

DIGITAL RIGHTS MANAGEMENT (DRM):
- Copy protection (prevent piracy)
- Patrons complain (difficult to use, format limits)
- Adobe Digital Editions (clunky software)

---

OPEN ACCESS:

PUBLIC DOMAIN:
- Copyright expired (pre-1928 in US)
- Free to use, distribute
- Project Gutenberg (70,000+ free ebooks)
- Internet Archive (digital library)

CREATIVE COMMONS:
- Author chooses license (allow free use with conditions)
- CC0 (public domain)
- CC BY (attribution required)
- CC BY-NC (non-commercial use only)

OPEN EDUCATIONAL RESOURCES (OER):
- Free textbooks (reduce student costs)
- OpenStax, LibreTexts
- Quality peer-reviewed content
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Patron-centered** - Serve community needs (not personal preferences)
2. **Access & equity** - Remove barriers (eliminate fines, extend hours, diverse collection)
3. **Lifelong learning** - Provide resources for all ages, interests
4. **Intellectual freedom** - Defend right to read (resist censorship)
5. **Digital & physical** - Hybrid model (both needed)
