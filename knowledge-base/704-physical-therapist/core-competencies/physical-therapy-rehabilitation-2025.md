## Initial Evaluation

```markdown
SUBJECTIVE (Patient History):

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
"What brings you in today?"
Example: "Low back pain for 3 months"

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
- Onset: When did it start? Sudden or gradual?
- Location: Where exactly? (Point to area)
- Duration: Constant or intermittent?
- Intensity: 0-10 pain scale
- Aggravating factors: What makes it worse?
- Relieving factors: What helps?
- Previous treatment: What have you tried?

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
- Prior injuries
- Surgeries
- Chronic conditions (diabetes, arthritis, etc.)
- Medications

FUNCTIONAL GOALS:
- What can't you do now that you want to do?
- "Return to running 3 miles"
- "Play with grandkids without pain"
- "Walk up stairs without holding railing"

---

OBJECTIVE (Physical Examination):

OBSERVATION:
- Posture (standing, sitting)
- Gait (walking pattern)
- Swelling, bruising
- Muscle atrophy

RANGE OF MOTION (ROM):
- Active ROM (patient moves)
- Passive ROM (therapist moves)
- Measured with goniometer (degrees)

Example (Shoulder flexion):
- Normal: 0-180Â°
- Patient: 0-120Â° (limited by pain)

STRENGTH TESTING (Manual Muscle Test 0-5):
0 - No contraction
1 - Flicker of contraction
2 - Movement with gravity eliminated
3 - Movement against gravity
4 - Movement against some resistance
5 - Normal strength

SPECIAL TESTS:
- Orthopedic tests for specific conditions
- Example: Neer's test (shoulder impingement)

PALPATION:
- Feel for tenderness, muscle spasm, temperature

---

ASSESSMENT:
Clinical impression based on findings

PLAN:
Treatment interventions & frequency
```

```markdown
ASSESSMENT TESTS:

STRAIGHT LEG RAISE (SLR):
- Patient supine, leg straight
- Therapist lifts leg
- Positive: Pain <70Â° (nerve root irritation)
- Reproduction of leg pain (not back pain)

SLUMP TEST:
- Patient sits, slumps forward
- Add neck flexion, extend knee
- Positive: Leg pain (neural tension)

---

TREATMENT:

ACUTE PHASE (0-2 weeks):
- Pain relief: Ice, TENS, gentle manual therapy
- Education: Avoid aggravating movements
- Activity modification: No prolonged sitting/bending
- Gentle ROM exercises

SUBACUTE PHASE (2-6 weeks):
- Core strengthening:
  * Bird dog
  * Dead bug
  * Plank (progress from knees to toes)
- Hip strengthening (glute bridges)
- McKenzie extension exercises (if centralizes pain)

CHRONIC PHASE (6+ weeks):
- Progressive loading:
  * Squats
  * Deadlifts (proper form!)
  * Carries (farmer's walk)
- Return to activity/sport
- Maintenance program

---

HOME EXERCISE PROGRAM:

CAT-COW (Lumbar mobility):
- Start on hands and knees
- Arch back (cow), then round back (cat)
- 10 reps, 2x daily

BIRD DOG (Core stability):
- On hands and knees
- Extend opposite arm and leg
- Hold 5 seconds, 10 reps each side

DEAD BUG (Core anti-extension):
- Lie on back, knees bent 90Â°
- Lower opposite arm and leg
- Keep back flat on floor
- 10 reps each side

GLUTE BRIDGE (Hip extension):
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Lift hips, squeeze glutes
- Hold 2 seconds, 15 reps
```

### Knee Pain

```markdown
PATELLOFEMORAL PAIN SYNDROME (Runner's Knee):

SYMPTOMS:
- Anterior knee pain
- Worse with stairs, squatting, prolonged sitting
- No swelling
```

## Manual Therapy Techniques

```markdown
SOFT TISSUE MOBILIZATION:

MYOFASCIAL RELEASE:
- Sustained pressure on tight muscles/fascia
- Hold 30-90 seconds
- Patient breathes deeply
- Example: Suboccipital release (neck tension)

TRIGGER POINT THERAPY:
- Identify trigger points (taut bands, referred pain)
- Apply ischemic compression
- Hold until pain decreases (30-60 sec)
- Common sites: Upper trap, levator scap, piriformis

INSTRUMENT-ASSISTED (IASTM):
- Graston, Hawk Grips tools
- Break down scar tissue, adhesions
- Followed by stretching

---

JOINT MOBILIZATION (Maitland Grades):

GRADE 1 & 2 (Pain relief):
- Small amplitude movements
- Within available range
- Stimulate mechanoreceptors (gate control theory)

GRADE 3 & 4 (Increase ROM):
- Large amplitude movements
- Into tissue resistance
- Stretches capsule

Example (Glenohumeral joint):
- Patient supine, arm in scaption
- Therapist stabilizes scapula
- Mobilize humerus (posterior glide)
- Improves flexion ROM

---

CONTRAINDICATIONS:

ABSOLUTE:
- Fracture
- Infection
- Malignancy
- Acute inflammation
- Hypermobility

RELATIVE:
- Osteoporosis (use caution, low grade)
- Pregnancy (avoid certain positions)
- Anticoagulants (avoid aggressive techniques)
```

## Exercise Prescription

```markdown
FITT PRINCIPLE:

FREQUENCY: How often?
- Strength: 2-3x/week (48-hour recovery)
- Flexibility: Daily
- Cardio: 3-5x/week

INTENSITY: How hard?
- Strength: 60-80% 1RM (10-15 reps)
- Cardio: 65-85% max HR (moderate to vigorous)

TIME: How long?
- Strength: 30-45 min
- Cardio: 30-60 min
- Stretching: Hold 30 seconds

TYPE: What kind?
- Strength: Resistance bands, weights, bodyweight
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Movement is medicine** - Active rehab > passive modalities
2. **Load management** - Progressive overload, avoid too much too soon
3. **Patient education** - Empowerment, self-management
4. **Functional goals** - Meaningful to patient's life
5. **Home exercise compliance** - Keep it simple, 3-5 exercises max
