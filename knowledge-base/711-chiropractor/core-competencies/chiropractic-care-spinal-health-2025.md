## Chiropractic Examination

```markdown
HISTORY:

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
- "What brings you in today?"
- Location (where hurts?)
- Onset (when start? sudden or gradual?)
- Duration (how long?)
- Intensity (0-10 scale)
- Aggravating factors (worse with sitting, bending, lifting?)
- Relieving factors (better with rest, ice, heat?)

RED FLAGS:
üö© Trauma (fall, car accident ‚Üí possible fracture)
üö© Age <20 or >50 (cancer more common)
üö© Fever, night sweats (infection)
üö© Weight loss (cancer)
üö© Bowel/bladder dysfunction (cauda equina ‚Üí emergency!)
üö© Progressive neurological deficit (weakness worsening)

If present ‚Üí Refer to MD, imaging (X-ray, MRI)

---

PHYSICAL EXAM:

POSTURE:
- Front view: Level shoulders, level hips
- Side view: Ear over shoulder over hip
- Scoliosis: Spine curves sideways (S or C shape)

RANGE OF MOTION (ROM):
CERVICAL (Neck):
- Flexion (chin to chest): 45-50¬∞
- Extension (look up): 45-50¬∞
- Lateral flexion (ear to shoulder): 40-45¬∞
- Rotation (turn head): 60-80¬∞

LUMBAR (Low back):
- Flexion (bend forward, touch toes): 80-90¬∞
- Extension (bend backward): 20-30¬∞
- Lateral flexion: 20-30¬∞

ORTHOPEDIC TESTS:

STRAIGHT LEG RAISE (SLR):
- Patient supine (on back)
- Lift leg straight up
- Positive: Pain radiates down leg <70¬∞ (disc herniation)

KEMP'S TEST (Low back):
- Patient stands, extends & rotates back
- Positive: Radiating leg pain (facet joint or nerve compression)

SPURLING'S TEST (Neck):
- Extend neck, rotate, apply downward pressure
- Positive: Arm pain/tingling (cervical nerve compression)

---

NEUROLOGICAL EXAM:

REFLEXES (grade 0-4):
- 0: Absent (nerve damage)
- 1+: Diminished
- 2+: Normal
- 3+: Brisk
- 4+: Hyperactive (upper motor neuron lesion)

Tests:
- Biceps (C5-C6)
- Patellar (knee-jerk, L3-L4)
- Achilles (ankle, S1)

SENSATION:
- Light touch (cotton swab)
- Pinprick
- Dermatomes (nerve distribution patterns)

MUSCLE STRENGTH (MMT 0-5):
- 5: Normal (full ROM against resistance)
- 4: Good (some resistance)
- 3: Fair (full ROM against gravity, no resistance)
- 2: Poor (ROM without gravity)
- 1: Trace (contraction visible, no movement)
- 0: None

Test key muscles:
- Shoulder abduction (C5)
- Wrist extension (C6)
- Elbow extension (C7)
- Hip flexion (L2)
- Knee extension (L3)
- Ankle dorsiflexion (L4)
- Great toe extension (L5)
```

## Spinal Adjustment Techniques

```markdown
HIGH-VELOCITY LOW-AMPLITUDE (HVLA):

DIVERSIFIED TECHNIQUE:
- Most common
- Quick thrust (audible "pop" or "crack")
- Specific joint

EXAMPLE (Lumbar side posture):
1. Patient side-lying
2. Bottom leg straight, top leg flexed
3. Rotate torso (opposite direction of legs)
4. Contact transverse process with pisiform
5. Quick thrust (P‚ÜíA, posterior to anterior)
6. "Pop" = gas release from joint (cavitation)

NOT PAINFUL (if done correctly):
- Pressure, then release
- If hurts, stop (wrong technique or contraindication)

---

LOW-FORCE TECHNIQUES:

ACTIVATOR:
- Handheld spring-loaded instrument
- Small, controlled impulse
- No "cracking"
- Good for: Elderly, osteoporosis, patient preference

SACRO-OCCIPITAL TECHNIQUE (SOT):
- Wedge blocks under pelvis
- Patient's body weight creates gentle adjustment
- Gravity-assisted
- Very gentle

DROP TABLE:
- Table with sections that drop slightly
- Momentum assists adjustment
- Less force needed

---

MOBILIZATION:

- Slow, repetitive movements
- No thrust, no "pop"
- Stretch joint, increase ROM
- Example: Cervical lateral flexion (gently pull head to side)

SOFT TISSUE:
- Massage (myofascial release)
- Trigger point therapy (pressure on knots)
- Instrument-assisted (Graston, scraping tool)
- Active Release Technique (ART): Move limb while applying pressure
```

## Common Conditions

```markdown
LOW BACK PAIN:

MECHANICAL (Non-specific):
- 85% of low back pain
- Muscle strain, ligament sprain
- Precipitating factor (lifted heavy, awkward position)
- No nerve involvement

TREATMENT:
- Spinal adjustment (restore motion)
- Soft tissue therapy (relax muscles)
- Ice (first 48 hours, reduce inflammation)
- Heat (after 48 hours, relax muscles)
- Exercise (core strengthening, avoid bed rest!)
- Expected: 50% better in 1 week, 90% in 6 weeks

---

DISC HERNIATION:

- Disc bulge ‚Üí presses nerve ‚Üí radiating pain
- Sciatica: Pain down back of leg (L5 or S1 nerve)
- Symptoms: Leg pain > back pain, numbness, tingling, weakness

TREATMENT:
- Flexion-distraction (Cox technique): Gentle stretch, decompress disc
- Avoid extension exercises (pushes disc further out)
- Nerve flossing (gentle nerve glides)
- 80% improve with conservative care (avoid surgery)
- Refer if: Progressive weakness, cauda equina

SURGERY (if needed):
- Microdiscectomy (remove disc fragment)
- Success rate: 80-90%
- Last resort (try 6-12 weeks conservative first)

---

NECK PAIN:

MECHANICAL (Facet joint):
- Stiff, ache
- Worse with extension, rotation
- Treatment: Adjustment, mobilization, posture correction

WHIPLASH (Car accident):
- Hyperextension ‚Üí hyperflexion
- Symptoms: Neck pain, headache, dizziness
- Treatment: Early mobilization (don't immobilize!), exercise
- Prognosis: 50% full recovery, 50% chronic pain

TEXT NECK (Tech neck):
- Forward head posture (looking down at phone)
- Headaches, upper back pain
- Treatment: Posture correction, chin tucks, strengthen neck flexors
- Prevention: Hold phone at eye level, take breaks

---

HEADACHES:

CERVICOGENIC (Neck-origin):
- Start in neck ‚Üí spread to head
- One-sided
- Worse with neck movement, pressure on neck muscles
- Treatment: Cervical adjustment, upper back mobilization
- Success rate: 70-80%

TENSION HEADACHE:
- Band-like pressure
- Bilateral (both sides)
- Stress, poor posture
- Treatment: Adjustment, soft tissue therapy, stress management

MIGRAINE:
- Severe, throbbing, one-sided
- Nausea, light sensitivity
- Chiropractic helps some (reduce frequency/intensity)
- Refer if: Frequent (>4/month), severe
```

## Rehabilitation & Exercise

```markdown
ACUTE PHASE (0-2 weeks):

GOALS:
- Reduce pain, inflammation
- Gentle ROM (avoid stiffness)

INTERVENTIONS:
- Ice (15 min, 3-4√ó daily)
- Avoid aggravating activities (no heavy lifting)
- Gentle stretches (pain-free range)

---

SUBACUTE PHASE (2-6 weeks):

GOALS:
- Restore ROM
- Begin strengthening

EXERCISES:

CAT-CAMEL (Lumbar mobility):
- Hands and knees
- Arch back (cat), then sag (camel)
- 10 reps, 2-3√ó daily

CHIN TUCKS (Neck posture):
- Sit/stand tall
- Pull chin straight back (double chin)
- Hold 5 sec, 10 reps

BIRD DOG (Core stability):
- Hands and knees
- Extend opposite arm and leg
- Hold 10 sec, 10 reps each side

---

CHRONIC PHASE (6+ weeks):

GOALS:
- Full ROM
- Strength, endurance
- Return to activity

EXERCISES:

PLANK (Core):
- Forearms and toes
- Hold 30-60 sec
- Progress to side plank

DEADLIFT (Posterior chain):
- Hip hinge (not squat)
- Keep back neutral
- Start light (bodyweight or dumbbells)

ROWS (Upper back):
- Pull shoulder blades together
- Strengthen postural muscles
- Counteract forward head posture

---

ERGONOMICS:

DESK SETUP:
- Monitor at eye level (not looking down)
- Elbows 90¬∞, wrists neutral
- Feet flat on floor
- Lumbar support (chair or cushion)
- Take breaks (stand every 30 min, walk every hour)

LIFTING:
- Bend knees, not back
- Keep load close to body
- No twisting while lifting
- Ask for help if heavy

SLEEPING:
- Side-lying: Pillow between knees
- Back: Pillow under knees
- Avoid stomach (twists neck)
- Pillow: Supports neck (not too high/low)
```

## Scope of Practice & Ethics

```markdown
WITHIN SCOPE:

‚úÖ Neuromusculoskeletal conditions (back, neck, joints)
‚úÖ Spinal adjustment, mobilization
‚úÖ Soft tissue therapy, exercise rehab
‚úÖ Lifestyle counseling (posture, ergonomics, nutrition basics)
‚úÖ Order imaging (X-ray, MRI)

OUTSIDE SCOPE:

‚ùå Prescribe medication (not medical doctors)
‚ùå Surgery
‚ùå Treat non-musculoskeletal (diabetes, cancer, infection)
‚ùå Obstetrics, pediatrics (without additional training)

CO-MANAGEMENT:
- Work with MDs, PTs, massage therapists
- Refer when appropriate (red flags, not improving, outside scope)
- Communication (send reports, collaborate on care)

---

CONTRAINDICATIONS (Don't adjust):

ABSOLUTE:
- Fracture (broken bone)
- Spinal cord compression (cauda equina, severe myelopathy)
- Infection (osteomyelitis, discitis)
- Malignancy (bone cancer, metastases)

RELATIVE (Modify technique):
- Osteoporosis (use low-force)
- Pregnancy (side-posture, avoid prone)
- Anticoagulants (bleeding risk, avoid forceful)
- Severe arthritis (gentle mobilization, avoid HVLA)

---

INFORMED CONSENT:

RISKS:
- Soreness (common, 24-48 hours)
- Stroke (very rare, 1 in 2 million cervical adjustments)
- Disc herniation (rare)
- Fracture (extremely rare, avoid with osteoporosis)

BENEFITS:
- Pain relief
- Improved function
- Avoid surgery/medication (many cases)

ALTERNATIVES:
- Physical therapy
- Medication (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants)
- Surgery (last resort)
- Do nothing (wait and see)

DOCUMENT: Patient signs consent form
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Evidence-based** - Use techniques with research support
2. **Patient-centered** - Goals, preferences, informed consent
3. **Avoid overclaiming** - Chiropractic helps musculoskeletal (not cure-all)
4. **Refer appropriately** - Red flags, not improving, outside scope
5. **Rehab & prevention** - Adjustment + exercise + ergonomics
